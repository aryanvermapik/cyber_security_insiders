Cyber Threats

CYBER MENACES IN THE MODERN WORLD

Cyber threats are an escalating global concern, posing significant risks to individuals, businesses, and governments. These threats encompass a range of malicious activities, including hacking, phishing, ransomware, and espionage. Hackers exploit vulnerabilities in systems to steal sensitive data, disrupt operations, and cause financial losses. The proliferation of the Internet of Things (IoT) and the increasing reliance on digital infrastructure have expanded the attack surface, making cybersecurity more challenging.

PHISHING

Phishing is a type of cyberattack where attackers disguise themselves as trustworthy entities to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information, such as passwords or credit card details. This is often done through deceptive emails or messages that appear legitimate. The goal is to steal personal data or install malicious software on the victim’s device


BRUTE FORCE ATTACK

A brute force attack is a hacking method that uses trial and error to guess passwords, login credentials, or encryption keys. Attackers systematically try all possible combinations until they find the correct one. This method is simple but can be time-consuming and is often automated.


MALWARE

Malware, short for malicious software, is designed to harm or exploit any programmable device, service, or network. Common types include viruses, ransomware, and spyware, which can steal data, disrupt operations, or gain unauthorized access to systems. Protecting against malware involves using antivirus software, firewalls, and regular system updates


VIRUS

A virus is a type of malware that attaches itself to a legitimate program or file, enabling it to spread from one computer to another. It can corrupt or delete data, use your email to spread itself to other computers, or even erase everything on your hard disk. Protecting against viruses involves using antivirus software, avoiding suspicious links, and keeping your system updated.


UNAUTHORISED SURVEILLANCE

Unauthorized surveillance involves monitoring individuals or groups without their consent, often violating privacy rights. It can include activities like wiretapping, hacking into cameras, or tracking online activities. Protecting against unauthorized surveillance requires using encryption, secure communication tools, and being cautious about sharing personal information.


DDOS ATTACKS

A Distributed Denial of Service (DDoS) attack involves overwhelming a target server, service, or network with a flood of internet traffic, causing it to become slow or completely unavailable. Attackers use multiple compromised devices to generate this traffic, making it difficult to stop. Protecting against DDoS attacks involves using firewalls, load balancers, and DDoS mitigation services.
